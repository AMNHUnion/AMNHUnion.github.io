Salary Increases:

  • The union negotiated an increased base salary for Museum Specialists, who make up approximately a third of the current Local 1559 membership.
  • The union contract specifies a career ladder for each job title, with salary raises at defined intervals according to experience attained and years worked. The union shortened time to raises for Museum Specialists.
  • In 2019, the union negotiated salary raises for the most junior Exhibition Preparators and negotiated a nearly $20K increase in the starting salary for our newest hire.
  • Work schedules, including both on-site and work-from-home (WFH) scheduling issues
Reduction in number of layoffs in Spring 2020 and earlier for union members:

  • Early in the pandemic, out of 91 union members, the union ensured that only 3 were laid off, instead of 17 targeted by the museum. In particular:
  • Two of the 11 Exhibition Preparators were targeted, but the union negotiated for them to keep their jobs.
  • The union moved members to other titles/depts to prevent layoffs.
  • The union also moved members between locals to prevent layoffs.
  • Several senior members were able to qualify for early retirement and left with a generous severance package.
  • Furthermore, the union contract prevents the museum from laying off the workers with the most seniority, and therefore the highest earnings.
